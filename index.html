What’s a Romance Scam?

Romance scams can be very cruel. They play on peoples emotions and vulnerabilities. A romance scammer will build up trust by developing a convincing relationship with you, usually online. You may meet them on a dating site or on social media where they’ll be lurking behind an apparently genuine profile. Once the scammer has gained your trust, they’ll start asking you to send money, vouchers or gifts.

Scammers are becoming increasingly sophisticated and will even find ways to get your personal details or passwords to access your bank account. They’ll invent reasons for needing money such as health issues, business issues or any other troubles they desperately need money to fix.

Warning Signs of a Romance Scammer

How Romance Scams Work?

Looking For Victims Online

A cybercriminal creates a fake profile on Facebook, Instagram & dating website's to court victims online. They ask a lot personal questions about you and then professes love quickly. You are one of many victims they simultaneously deceive.

Asks Victims For Money

Victims have already bought into the fairytale of finding “the one” so they want to believe it's true. The relationship moves fast and trust is gained. They may even ask you to marry them and promise a future together but then they start to ask for money and lure you off the dating site.

Scammer Takes Money & Vanishes

They always have an excuse for you to send them money or expensive items so that you’ll offer to help. They take advantage of your heart and good will. They will you for money over and over again until you have no more to send and then they vanish.
